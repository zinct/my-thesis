\chapter*{ABSTRACT}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ABSTRACT}

Asset management, inventory, and rental services across multiple organizations operating on a
single digital platform are still often implemented using a non-multitenant approach. In this
approach, each organization is managed through separate system configurations, leading to limited
scalability, high operational complexity, and potential data inconsistency as the number of users
and organizations grows at a national scale.

This issue is important to address because multi-organization web-based service platforms are
designed to serve multiple entities in a centralized manner, making conventional architectural
approaches no longer adequate. In this study, a tenant is defined as an independent organizational
entity that has its own data, users, and operational processes, which must be logically isolated
within a shared system.

This research proposes a multitenant architecture design based on a shared database and shared
schema as a solution to improve infrastructure efficiency, maintain data isolation between tenants,
and support the growth of organizations at a national scale. The research methodology includes
system requirements analysis, the design of tenant scoping mechanisms at the application layer,
and the design of automated tenant provisioning processes.

The evaluation of the proposed design is conducted based on parameters such as the ability to
ensure data isolation, the effectiveness of tenant provisioning without manual configuration, and
the consistency of asset, inventory, and rental management functions across multiple tenants.

The expected outcome is a relevant and feasible multitenant architecture design for
multi-organization service platforms at a national scale, capable of overcoming the limitations
of non-multitenant approaches in similar systems.

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} multitenant architecture, shared schema, tenant scoping,
tenant provisioning, multi organization platform, asset and rental management system
